Computer Science

The Faculty of Computing, formerly known as the Faculty of Computer Systems & Software Engineering, was established on February 16, 2002, to produce knowledgeable, highly skilled and competitive graduates in software engineering, systems, and computer networks. Initially, the faculty offered two fields of study: Software Engineering and Networking.

The faculty has actively engaged in research and development across various domains, including software engineering, computer systems, communication systems, and multimedia technology to develop industry-relevant technologies. Currently, the faculty hosts several research groups such as Cyber Security Interest Group (Cy-SIG), Educational Technology (Edutech), Software Engineering (SERG), Soft Computing & Optimization (SCORE), Machine Intelligence Research Group (MIRG), and Data Science & Simulation Modeling (DSSIM).

The faculty emphasizes on the development and growth of its student's enrolment and graduates. Through high quality teaching, lab facilities, and professional activities, students have the opportunity to excel in classroom and laboratory sessions.
